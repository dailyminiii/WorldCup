\section{Results}

\subsection{Sample construction}
The source contained \InitialWindows{} team--match five-minute windows from \MatchCount{} matches and \TeamCount{} teams. After the common score-state, period, and numerical-state restrictions and the opponent-pass threshold, \IntensityWindows{} windows entered the intensity model. The sequence denominator restrictions retained \EfficiencyWindows{} windows for the efficiency model. The stepwise exclusion table reconciles every reduction; no match or team was silently removed. Drawing contributed the largest exposure total, while leading and trailing exposure seconds were symmetric in the fixed-window construction. Opportunity totals by state are shown in Table~1.

\subsection{Descriptive results}
Pooled Pressure events per 30 opponent passes were \DescriptiveIntensityLeadingPooled{} when leading, \DescriptiveIntensityDrawingPooled{} when drawing, and \DescriptiveIntensityTrailingPooled{} when trailing. The corresponding unweighted mean window rates were \DescriptiveIntensityLeadingMean{}, \DescriptiveIntensityDrawingMean{}, and \DescriptiveIntensityTrailingMean{}. These differ because pooled rates weight windows by opponent-pass exposure, whereas unweighted summaries give each eligible window equal weight.

Pooled five-second sequence-regain proportions were \DescriptiveEfficiencyLeadingPooled{}, \DescriptiveEfficiencyDrawingPooled{}, and \DescriptiveEfficiencyTrailingPooled{} for leading, drawing, and trailing. Unweighted mean window proportions were \DescriptiveEfficiencyLeadingMean{}, \DescriptiveEfficiencyDrawingMean{}, and \DescriptiveEfficiencyTrailingMean{}. These are unadjusted summaries and should not be read as model-standardized comparisons.

\subsection{Primary pressing-intensity model}
Relative to drawing, trailing was associated with a higher adjusted Pressure-event rate: IRR \IntensityTrailingEffect{} (log-rate coefficient \IntensityTrailingCoef{}, 95\% CI \IntensityTrailingCILow{} to \IntensityTrailingCIHigh{}). Predicted Pressure events per 30 opponent passes were \IntensityTrailingPrediction{} when trailing and \IntensityDrawingPrediction{} when drawing, an adjusted difference of approximately \IntensityTrailingDifference\%. Leading versus drawing had IRR \IntensityLeadingEffect{} (coefficient \IntensityLeadingCoef{}, 95\% CI \IntensityLeadingCILow{} to \IntensityLeadingCIHigh{}); predicted rates were \IntensityLeadingPrediction{} and \IntensityDrawingPrediction{}, respectively, an adjusted difference of approximately \IntensityLeadingDifference\%.

\subsection{Primary regain-efficiency model}
Trailing versus drawing had an estimated sequence-regain odds ratio of \EfficiencyTrailingEffect{} (log-odds coefficient \EfficiencyTrailingCoef{}, 95\% CI \EfficiencyTrailingCILow{} to \EfficiencyTrailingCIHigh{}). Adjusted predicted probabilities were \EfficiencyTrailingPrediction{} and \EfficiencyDrawingPrediction{}, a \EfficiencyTrailingDifference{} percentage-point difference. Leading versus drawing had OR \EfficiencyLeadingEffect{} (coefficient \EfficiencyLeadingCoef{}, 95\% CI \EfficiencyLeadingCILow{} to \EfficiencyLeadingCIHigh{}); predicted probabilities were \EfficiencyLeadingPrediction{} and \EfficiencyDrawingPrediction{}, a \EfficiencyLeadingDifference{} percentage-point difference.

\subsection{Secondary and exploratory results}
High-pressure share and event-level regain models supplied secondary checks on location and measurement unit. Their estimated trailing contrasts were positive, while the event and sequence estimands remained distinct. Provider counterpress share, high-regain rate, Pressure height, sequence frequency, and classic defensive-action counts are retained in Table~3 rather than selected by statistical appearance. Post-regain shot production is secondary; xG and xT per successful regain are exploratory. Same-possession goal is explicitly unavailable, and provider-augmented PPDA remains an unavailable sensitivity entry in the inferential table.
