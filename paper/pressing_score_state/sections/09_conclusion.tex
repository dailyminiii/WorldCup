\section{Conclusion}

This study separates pressing intensity from pressure-to-regain efficiency and post-regain value in the 2022 FIFA World Cup. Relative to drawing, trailing was associated with higher adjusted Pressure-event frequency per opponent pass and higher estimated five-second sequence-regain odds; leading contrasts were smaller and negative. The results do not identify a score-state mechanism. Their main contribution is a denominator-aware, sequence-aware, reproducible framework that makes measurement and robustness choices explicit. Future work can test these relationships in broader competitions and, separately, examine dynamic qualification pressure without treating the present tournament associations as universal.
